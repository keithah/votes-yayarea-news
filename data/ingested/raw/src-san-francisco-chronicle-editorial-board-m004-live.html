S.F. Chronicle endorsements for the 2026 California Primary Election

The San Francisco Chronicle Editorial Board’s endorsements for the California and S.F. Bay Area primary election on June 2.

By Chronicle Editorial Board | Updated May 16, 2026 7:49 a.m.

Every election season, California voters not only have to deal with a glut of campaign promises from candidates, we also must navigate ballot measures written by special interests and local races that take place in an informational vacuum. Here in the Bay Area, many voters in 2026 will manage all this against the backdrop of a Trump administration that has changed the political landscape — and reduced funding for state and local programs.

In this kind of environment, details matter. And, yes, your vote matters.

The San Francisco Chronicle editorial board has spent months interviewing candidates, talking to experts, fact checking campaign promises and scrutinizing the issues in every statewide contest on the ballot, along with a group of consequential races in the Bay Area. These recommendations are made by Opinion writers and editors; newsroom reporters who cover the campaigns do not participate in endorsement decisions.

Below are the editorial board’s recommendations for the June 2026 primary; we will continue to roll out more in coming weeks. We encourage readers to click through to the full endorsements to see the board’s reasoning and understand its decisions — so that you can be fully armed to make your own informed decisions.

California races
The editorial board recommends
Governor: Katie PorterDemocrat

Consumer protection advocate

Katie Porter is largely known for two things: her aggressive questioning of CEOs using her famous whiteboard and her infamous yelling at a staffer that was caught on video. Ultimately, both are superficial reasons to support or dismiss her candidacy. Unlike our current governor, we believe Porter would be laser-focused on Californians, not on her next job. Her combination of policy smarts, political courage and independence puts her in the best position to pursue the evidence-based solutions California needs to thrive.

Read the full editorial

Lieutenant Governor: Michael TubbsDemocrat

Anti-poverty nonprofit director

California lieutenant governor sounds like a powerful and high-profile job, but the role is really a "backwater." Michael Tubbs will know what to do with it.

Read the full editorial

Secretary of State: Shirley WeberDemocrat

California Secretary of State ▪ Incumbent

California is no ocean of calm when it comes to elections. The need for steady and trustworthy leadership from our secretary of state cannot be understated. Shirley Weber’s breadth of experience, willingness to do the right thing and fierce advocacy for voting rights make her the strongest candidate.

The California state treasurer is the main banker, financier and asset manager for the world’s fourth-largest economy. With her thorough grasp of the treasurer’s complex duties and the political gravitas to get things done, Eleni Kounalakis deserves your vote.

Attorney general ▪ Incumbent

In our endorsement interview, incumbent Rob Bonta’s calm, clear and concise grasp of the complexities and nuances of California law — honed through more than eight years in the state Assembly — surpassed that of most of the current gubernatorial candidates.

Insurance Commissioner: Patrick WolffDemocrat

Financial analyst

California needs someone to do the hard work of competently regulating the insurance system before we can fully solve its shortcomings. Patrick Wolff is up for it.

Superintendent of Public Instruction: Richard Barrera

School board member

With nearly two decades of experience governing California’s second-largest school district, Barrera has an encyclopedic knowledge of education policy and a track record and obvious passion for helping students succeed. We believe Barrera is uniquely qualified to get diverse stakeholders rowing in the same direction to improve outcomes for kids.

Alameda County
The editorial board recommends
Alameda County Superior Court Judge, Office No. 13: Michael Johnson

Johnson has extensive legal experience in the civil and criminal realms — and on the bench. Over the past seven years, he has handled traffic, small claims, civil harassment and family law cases at Alameda Superior Court, and he has earned the endorsements of several fellow judges.

Deputy city attorney

Measured and thoughtful, Warren boasts impressive endorsements from sitting Superior Court judges and the Alameda County Democratic Party, as well as political support from several Oakland City Council members and labor groups like the Service Employees International Union.

Oakland ballot measures
The editorial board recommends
Oakland Measure C: Yes

Small businesses are the backbone of Oakland’s economy. They desperately need a tax break in a time of tariffs and inflation. Measure C is a modest and pragmatic effort that recognizes the importance of nurturing local enterprise from the ground up.
